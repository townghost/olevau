My hysterectomy

I had a hysterectomy like, two days ago. I’m supposed to take my testosterone yesterday.

I’m very private about this because most of the trans community doesn’t support me, they don’t think that being transsexual is a medical condition.

I think it is. I just don’t think there’s anything wrong with that.

I like the disability community. Communities around things like autism and ADHD make me feel much more safe than the political movement surrounding the trans issue.

I feel like what i’m trying to describe about myself is best described by the spiritual. The pure awareness that they talk about, that’s what i feel like i’m trying to describe to my community, endlessly.

It seems like everyone’s ready with a way to explain away my existence.

Like there’s no way to win at being openly transsexual. There’s no way to explain myself. So this motivates my transition. Like, i’ll never escape from being observed. The only way to exist is to perform myself.

I used to run with a bunch of radical feminists, because they were the only people i saw talking about the horror of being observed. But in exchange they demanded the dissolution of myself so i was again without answers.

You know… gender is a disease of the mind. Like racism, it’s a core assumption about the character of who you observe. Even in a close relationship, it’s hard to get past the body.

You know in this world, there are humans who would seek to punish people like me with forced impregnation. It’s a fetish. A little bit of my joy is depriving them of that.

My feelings toward my reproductive system are a little bit soiled by the memories of my mother exposing herself to me. Some kind of feminist act, some kind of sign i probably shouldn’t ever deal with feminism during the course of my life.

The cult of it at least, the idea that women can never abuse or do wrong, or that the idea of femaleness doesn’t contain a little stagnation and disease.

Since i am a transsexual, i moved to san francisco to get my surgeries, try to get a job and live healthily. But i’m so much more than that. I’m a human. I have needs and desires equal to anyone else.

Being Human

The state of being human, what comes with being in a human body, without all the bells and whistles of it. I’ve thought extensively about it, but i still don’t think i know --?

Being human comes with -- searching for food. Searching for warmth. Searching for other humans, some humans that match in some way. That act in a way you can understand.

The people of the streets are so enthusiastic to come together to pool the results of their wealth.

I was homeless for a while. I lived in the state of being human without all the bells and whistles of it. I still had some of the bells. I was raised by a mother. At some point, i had a home and food. I was educated.

I was trying to tell a story. That time, i wanted to tell some kind of story to somebody about who i am. I didn’t want to passively let people observe me and tell some kind of story about who i am. All of this time, hidden needs were working inside me. I looked like a monkey. I looked for food.

I thought i needed to know somebody, but all i needed was to be alone. I was telling the story to myself, over and over, so i wouldn’t forget who i am.

The knife

The knife is one of the most important tools of the human.

Without a knife, a human is missing a limb.

To go under the knife is to get a surgery. To let another human cut you to help you.

A knife is safety and trust. I want to live “under the knife”.

The city

A city is a settlement of humans. People work together to pool the results of their wealth. With their wealth, the people have allowed me to go under the knife, to cut me in order to heal me.

The city, the spirits, and the knife

I used to carry a knife. I used to be very forgetful and would just keep forgetting and losing my knife. I imagined a reason for this scenario. I imagined that i was part of a secret tribe in the city that was watching me closely. These people took away my knife, so i would learn to be soft. I would learn to be safe without the threat of pain. Or maybe they were spirits. Maybe the people weren’t here in this physical plane, but i imagined this reason why, and i stopped carrying a knife.
